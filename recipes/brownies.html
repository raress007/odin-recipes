Brownies (24 portions)

Description

Brownies

Prep: 10 min | Total: 50 min | Serves: 24 pieces | Difficulty: Easy

Rich, dense, and deeply chocolatey, these brownies are built on a base of 70% dark chocolate and butter, sweetened with sugar and bound with six eggs for that characteristic fudgy texture. The batter comes together in just 10 minutes, with optional walnuts adding a satisfying crunch to each square.

Simple enough for a beginner but impressive enough to share, they cut into 24 generous pieces, perfect for a bake sale, an after-school treat, or whenever a serious chocolate craving strikes.

Ingredients

  • 220g butter, unsalted (cut in pieces, plus extra for greasing)
  • 250g dark chocolate, 70% cocoa solids (cut in pieces)
  • 380g sugar
  • 6 eggs
  • 1 pinch salt
  • 150g flour
  • 150g shelled halved walnuts (optional)

Tips

  • If using chocolate with a lower cocoa percentage, increase chocolate and decrease sugar amount. For instance, for chocolate with 50% cocoa, increase chocolate to 360 g and decrease sugar to 260 g.

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 170°C. Grease a cake tin (approx. 30 cm x 20 cm) and line with baking paper, letting edges of paper hang over sides of tin (the excess baking paper will serve as handles to lift out the brownies).
  2. Place chocolate in mixing bowl and chop 5 sec/speed 7.
  3. Add butter and melt 4 min/70°C/speed 2.
  4. Add sugar, eggs, salt and flour and mix 1 min/speed 4. Scrape down sides of mixing bowl using spatula.
  5. Add walnuts and mix 10 sec/speed 4.
  6. Transfer to prepared cake tin, distributing batter evenly. Bake for 23-25 minutes (170°C), or until a toothpick or skewer inserted in the centre comes out with a few damp crumbs. Remove from cake tin using the baking paper to lift out the brownies. Allow to cool and serve brownies cut in squares.
